Poster / Broadside · Concept

Scale Is the Hierarchy.

The rule
One statement, one scale jump, no boxes. Hierarchy comes entirely from how large the headline is relative to everything else on the page — never from color, borders, or a shadowed container.
Best for
Cover pages, section dividers, title slides — a single moment, not a page of content. It adapts directly and strongly to a slide's title or section-divider layout.
Never
A second real idea. The instant a page needs a second paragraph, a second box, or a chart, this archetype has been outgrown — reach for magazine-well or single-column-editorial instead.
