What Does IREN Actually Do?

The Simple Story

Imagine you built a giant warehouse full of supercomputers in Texas and British Columbia, and you powered it with wind and solar. At first, you rented those computers to people who wanted to earn Bitcoin — like a massive digital gold mine. Now, the same buildings are being refitted for a new tenant: artificial intelligence. Microsoft and NVIDIA are paying IREN billions to use its computers to train and run AI models. IREN provides the power, the building, and the hardware — the AI companies bring the software and pay the rent.

The Big Bet Right Now

In 2026, IREN's single biggest bet is executing on its $9.7 billion Microsoft contract at its 750-megawatt Childress, Texas campus. This requires building brand-new liquid-cooled data centers (because AI chips run hotter than Bitcoin miners), installing a 1.4 gigawatt power substation, and deploying tens of thousands of NVIDIA GB300 "Blackwell" graphics cards. The company secured a $3.65 billion investment-grade financing facility in June 2026 to fund this buildout — a milestone that transforms IREN's credit profile and removes the biggest question mark over execution.

Business Model

How They Make Money

  • Bitcoin Mining (77% today): Run ASIC computers 24/7 to earn Bitcoin as a reward for processing transactions. Revenue scales with Bitcoin price and network hashrate.
  • AI Cloud Services (23% today, growing fast): Rent clusters of NVIDIA GPUs to AI companies (Microsoft, NVIDIA) on multi-year contracts at high margins. Contracted ARR: $3.1B.
  • Long-term: Managed Services: NVIDIA deal is structured as a "managed services" contract — IREN operates the hardware, NVIDIA customers access it remotely.
  • Energy Arbitrage: IREN builds in regions with cheap renewable power. When electricity costs drop, mining margins expand. This same cost advantage applies to AI data center economics.

Why the Model Is Clever

  • Shared infrastructure: Data centers serve both Bitcoin miners and AI — same buildings, same power, different hardware. Switching between uses is possible as economics shift.
  • Long-term contracts lock in cash flows: 5-year deals with Microsoft and NVIDIA convert volatile Bitcoin income into predictable subscription-like revenue.
  • 100% renewable energy mandate: ESG credentials and lower carbon cost attract institutional investors and premium AI customers who have sustainability commitments.
  • Vertical integration: IREN owns the land, builds the infrastructure, procures the GPUs, and operates the data centers — capturing all margin layers rather than splitting with a landlord or co-location operator.
Hidden Catalyst Deep Dive — The Investment-Grade Re-Rating [EDITORIAL OPINION]

What Most Investors Are Missing

Everyone knows about the Microsoft and NVIDIA contracts. What the market has not fully processed is what closing the $3.65 billion investment-grade GPU financing facility in June 2026 actually means. This facility is backed by the Microsoft AI Cloud contract — meaning IREN is now borrowing money at rates reserved for A-rated corporate debt, not speculative miners. That is a category change, not just a funding event. A Bitcoin miner typically pays 12–15% for debt; an investment-grade infrastructure company pays 4–7%. The lower cost of capital alone dramatically improves the return on every GPU deployed.

Why IREN Has an Edge Here

Very few companies can credibly sign a $9.7B contract with Microsoft and then use that contract as collateral for investment-grade financing. IREN's edge is that it holds both the power capacity (3+ GW secured) and the physical land in regions with cheap renewable energy — something that takes years to replicate. Competitors like Core Scientific and Riot Platforms lack comparable contracted ARR at this scale and do not have NVIDIA as a direct equity-aligned partner (NVIDIA holds warrants at $70/share, making them financially incentivized to see IREN succeed). The NVIDIA warrant structure is a publicly disclosed vote of confidence with a dollar amount attached.

Investor Debate [EDITORIAL OPINION]

The Optimist Sees...

  • $13.1B in contracted AI cloud revenue gives IREN a revenue floor that most infrastructure stocks would envy — this is not speculative; it is signed paper.
  • At ~$55 stock price versus $3.1B contracted ARR, IREN trades at less than 7x forward contracted revenue — a discount to cloud infrastructure peers trading at 15–25x.
  • NVIDIA as an equity-aligned partner (holding $70 warrants on 30M shares) is unprecedented for a company this size and signals that NVIDIA believes IREN is a long-term winner in AI infrastructure.
  • The investment-grade GPU financing closed in June 2026 de-risks the biggest concern: can IREN fund the buildout? Yes, and at institutional rates.
  • Bitcoin mining is not dead weight — it generates $100M+ per quarter in cash flow that funds the transition without burning equity, something pure-play AI infrastructure startups cannot do.

The Pessimist Sees...

  • Q3 FY2026 missed revenue estimates by $75M — 34% below consensus. For a company at this growth stage, missing by that margin is a warning sign about execution discipline.
  • Cash fell from $3.3B to $2.2B in a single quarter. The $5.8B Dell purchase commitment is real money that has to come from somewhere — and dilution is the most likely source if AI revenue ramp slips.
  • 77% Bitcoin mining revenue means one bad quarter in crypto wipes out a year of AI progress in the income statement. This is still fundamentally a commodity exposure story wearing AI clothing.
  • The Culper Research short seller report from July 2024 was never formally refuted — it questioned whether IREN's AI pivot was credible. With $13.1B in contracts, the thesis looks weaker, but the company still has not reached profitability.
  • Building 750MW of liquid-cooled data center capacity and deploying 150,000 GPUs simultaneously is a task that has defeated far larger, more experienced companies. On-time execution is an assumption, not a certainty.
Bottom Line Verdict [EDITORIAL OPINION]
The Verdict — Runway Meets Rocket, But the Runway Is Still Being Paved

Think of IREN as a regional airport that successfully lured two of the world's biggest airlines — Microsoft and NVIDIA — to sign 5-year gate leases before the runways are fully finished. The deals are real, the contracts are signed, and the money is in escrow. But if the construction crew hits delays, those airlines will sit on the tarmac while the investors wait on the observation deck wondering when the first flight departs.

The long-term story is believable. IREN has genuine scarcity assets: 3+ gigawatts of secured renewable power, vertically integrated data centers in Texas and British Columbia, and a June 2026 investment-grade financing facility that none of its crypto-mining peers can match. At $55 per share versus $3.1B in already-contracted ARR and analyst targets averaging $80+, the market is pricing in meaningful execution risk — and for good reason. But if milestones print, the re-rating from "miner" to "AI infrastructure" could push IREN toward the $90–$110 bull case by 2027.

The near-term pain is real. Q3 FY2026 missed consensus by 34%, Bitcoin still drives 77% of current revenue, the company burns cash at a rapid rate, and $5.8B in equipment payments loom. This is a stock for investors with a 2–3 year horizon who believe IREN's management can execute one of the most ambitious infrastructure buildouts in recent history. If you need profitability in the next 12 months, look elsewhere. The next event to watch: Q4 FY2026 earnings (expected September 2026) and the first Childress liquid-cooled milestone update.
