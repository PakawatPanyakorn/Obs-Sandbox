What Does Nebius Group Actually Do?

The Simple Story

Imagine every AI company in the world needs a giant, very fast calculator to run their smart software. Building those calculators requires thousands of special chips made by a company called NVIDIA, enormous amounts of electricity, and buildings big enough to hold everything.

Nebius builds and owns those buildings. Companies like Meta (which owns Facebook and Instagram) and Microsoft pay Nebius a monthly fee to use the space and the chips. Nebius handles the construction, the electricity, the cooling, and all the complicated computer plumbing — so AI companies can just plug in and get to work.

The Big Bet Right Now

Nebius is making one of the biggest infrastructure bets of the AI era: spending $20–25 billion in a single year to build AI factories across the US and Europe before demand peaks. It has already signed contracts worth roughly $50 billion from Meta and Microsoft — meaning the customers are locked in before the buildings are finished.

To pay for this, Nebius sold bonds, accepted a $2B check from NVIDIA itself, and is collecting billions in advance payments from customers. The question every investor asks: can Nebius build fast enough, and will AI demand still be surging when the buildings open?

Business Model

How They Make Money

●
GPU Cloud Rentals
Companies pay per GPU-hour or under long-term capacity contracts to train and run AI models. This accounts for roughly 97.5% of current revenue.
●
Inference-as-a-Service (Token Factory)
Via the newly acquired Eigen AI, Nebius sells optimized inference endpoints. Companies pay per "token" — the AI equivalent of a word — generated by hosted models.
●
Long-Term Capacity Contracts
Multi-year locked-in deals with Meta ($27B) and Microsoft ($19.4B) function like long-term real estate leases, providing predictable recurring revenue.
●
Education & AI Data Services
TripleTen (online coding bootcamps) and Toloka (AI data labeling) contribute minor but cash-generative revenue alongside the core cloud business.

Why the Model Is Clever

★
Signed Before Built
Nebius collects billions in advance (deferred revenue jumped from $1.58B to $4.78B in Q1 2026 alone) — customers fund the construction before it is complete.
★
NVIDIA as Backer and Supplier
NVIDIA's $2B equity stake is not just a check — it signals preferential GPU allocation in a market where H200/B200 chips are rationed. Nebius is effectively a priority customer of the world's most in-demand product.
★
Sovereign European Alternative
Netherlands-incorporated with Finnish data centers cleared by both US and EU regulators — the only hyperscale-capacity option satisfying EU AI Act data sovereignty requirements.
★
Margin Leverage Is Working
Cost of revenues fell from 49% to 26% of total revenue in just one year, demonstrating the operating leverage inherent in filling a fixed-cost infrastructure with paying customers.
Hidden Catalyst Deep Dive: Avride & Physical AI

What Most Analysts Are Missing

Every research note about Nebius focuses on GPU racks and data center square footage. Almost nobody writes about Avride — Nebius's autonomous vehicle and sidewalk delivery robot subsidiary — which operates as an independent business with its own funding, its own Uber partnership, and its own growth trajectory.

Avride completed 174,000 robot deliveries in Q1 2026 alone (+178% YoY), crossed 500,000 total deliveries since launch, and began operating commercial robotaxi service in Dallas in December 2025. None of this appears in consensus Nebius earnings models. It is essentially a free embedded call option on physical AI.

Why Nebius Has a Structural Advantage Here

Avride is the only autonomous vehicle company that also owns the cloud infrastructure it trains its models on. While Waymo and every other competitor pays AWS, Google Cloud, or Azure enormous bills to train self-driving AI, Avride runs all training on Nebius GPUs — cutting costs and accelerating iteration.

Nebius also launched the Physical AI Living Lab in the UK and Europe, giving robotics startups six months of free GPU access and NVIDIA tools. This seeds an entire generation of physical AI companies that will eventually become paying Nebius cloud customers — a customer acquisition strategy hidden inside a philanthropy announcement.

Investor Debate [EDITORIAL OPINION]

The Optimist Sees...

  • A $50B contracted backlog de-risks execution risk entirely — Nebius already has the customers. The job is just to finish building the infrastructure they have already paid for.
  • Revenue growing 684% YoY with EBITDA turning positive at the same time is a once-in-a-generation operating leverage curve. The math gets dramatically better as utilization rates climb.
  • NVIDIA's $2B equity investment is the ultimate validator — the world's most powerful semiconductor company chose Nebius as its preferred infrastructure expansion partner over hundreds of alternatives.
  • Customers are financing Nebius's construction: the $3.2B jump in deferred revenue in Q1 2026 alone is essentially an interest-free loan from Meta and Microsoft to build the very assets Nebius will rent back to them. It is structurally brilliant.
  • Avride, Eigen AI, and the Physical AI Living Lab are three call options on massive markets that are not currently priced into the stock at all — free upside embedded in the share price.

The Pessimist Sees...

  • A company spending $20–25B in a single year while generating $3–3.4B in revenue. Every infrastructure sector — railroads, fiber, solar — has had its overbuild moment. Why is AI different?
  • Shares are already 30% more diluted since the 2024 relisting, and a $10–15B remaining funding gap guarantees more dilution ahead. Each capital raise reshuffles the deck against existing shareholders.
  • The stock at $277 already trades above most analyst price targets. Generating meaningful returns from here requires the company to beat already-optimistic expectations for years in a row.
  • Material weaknesses in a company doing $400M per quarter suggest financial controls are lagging physical buildout. Rapid growth plus weak internal controls has a long and unhappy track record.
  • Meta and Microsoft are writing $50B in contracts partly because Nebius is cheap today — not because they could not eventually build their own. Pricing power erodes as hyperscaler self-sufficiency grows.
Bottom Line Verdict [EDITORIAL OPINION]
The Verdict

Nebius is like a toll road company building the highway in the middle of the AI gold rush. The gold miners — Meta and Microsoft — are already lined up at the gate and have handed Nebius the construction funds. The road will almost certainly be used; the signed contracts say so. The question is whether Nebius can lay $20 billion worth of asphalt fast enough, and whether a larger highway (built by Amazon or Google themselves) does not open a parallel route and undercut the tolls before the first road fully pays for itself.

The long-term bull case is genuinely compelling. A $50B contracted backlog, 684% revenue growth, first-ever positive EBITDA, a $2B NVIDIA endorsement, and three additional option-value businesses (Eigen AI, Avride, Physical AI Living Lab) that no analyst currently models — this is a rare combination. The Eigen acquisition in particular could structurally expand margins from 40% to 50%+ as software revenues mix in. And Avride is a free embedded call option on the $7.6B autonomous delivery market that only Nebius shareholders hold.

The near-term bear case is equally credible: the company requires roughly $10–15B more in external capital during 2026, shares are 30% diluted from relisting, and a $277 stock price already exceeds most published analyst targets — meaning the stock needs to beat even the optimists to deliver further gains from here. Internal control weaknesses add governance risk on top of capital risk. Best suited for patient investors with a three-to-five year horizon who can tolerate significant dilution and sharp drawdowns in exchange for exposure to the most consequential infrastructure build of the AI decade.
