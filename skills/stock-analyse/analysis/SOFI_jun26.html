What Does This Company Actually Do?

The Simple Story

Imagine having one app on your phone that could do everything a bank does — give you a loan for school or a house, hold your savings and pay you interest, let you buy stocks, and even issue you a credit card. That is SoFi. Unlike Chase or Wells Fargo, SoFi has no physical bank branches. It does everything through your phone, which means lower costs and friendlier rates. The more products a member uses, the cheaper it is for SoFi to serve them — think of it like a gym membership that gets more valuable the more classes you take.

The Big Bet Right Now

SoFi is betting that its 14.7 million members will each use more products over time — a strategy called "cross-sell." Right now, 43% of new financial products opened each quarter come from existing members, which means SoFi is converting its members into multi-product customers. At the same time, SoFi is pivoting toward fee-based services (investing, stablecoins, crypto) that generate revenue without putting loans on its balance sheet. If this capital-light mix shift succeeds, profit margins could expand dramatically through 2028.

Business Model

How They Make Money

  • Interest income on personal, student, and home loans originated and held on balance sheet
  • Loan Platform Business fees — selling loans to third-party institutional buyers without holding credit risk
  • Banking fees from SoFi Money deposit accounts and SoFi Credit Card interchange
  • Investment management fees and brokerage commissions via SoFi Invest
  • Technology licensing fees from Galileo and Technisys — sold to other fintech companies
  • Stablecoin yield spread from SoFiUSD (launched May 2026)

Why the Model Is Clever

  • Member acquisition cost is amortized across more products — the flywheel gets cheaper per member over time
  • $40B in member deposits provides cheap funding for loans, widening net interest margin versus non-bank lenders
  • National bank charter (granted 2022) gives SoFi regulatory legitimacy and deposit insurance that pure fintechs lack
  • Galileo powers over 100 fintech companies — SoFi earns B2B infrastructure fees while competing with those same clients in B2C
  • Capital-light fee revenue is growing faster than lending, reducing balance-sheet risk without sacrificing revenue growth
Hidden Catalyst Deep Dive

The Opportunity

SoFiUSD — launched May 2026 — is the first stablecoin (a digital coin always worth exactly $1) issued by a US national bank. It lives on Ethereum and Solana blockchains, earns members 4.2% annual yield, and is redeemable 1:1 for dollars at any time. Future roadmap includes tokenized deposits and cross-border payments. Unlike crypto-native stablecoins like USDC, SoFiUSD comes with FDIC-adjacent bank trust and can be distributed to all 14.7 million existing SoFi members with no new customer acquisition cost.

Why This Company Wins

No major US bank has done this yet. JP Morgan has its own blockchain but only for institutional clients. Coinbase and Circle (USDC) are not banks and cannot offer insured deposits. SoFi's national bank charter gives it the legal authority that crypto-native firms lack, while its app-based distribution gives it 14.7 million retail wallets that no bank-issued stablecoin competitor currently has. Additionally, SoFi was named in the SpaceX IPO prospectus as a potential retail distribution broker — the first high-profile deal that proves SoFi can compete with Fidelity and Schwab for marquee IPO allocations.

Bottom Line Verdict [EDITORIAL OPINION]
The Verdict

SoFi is like a runner who just qualified for the Olympics — the talent is undeniable, the results are real, but there is an injury nobody has fully diagnosed yet. The business is growing fast and is now genuinely profitable, yet two unresolved legal investigations are doing exactly what short sellers want: keeping the stock below its fair value while the bulls wait for a green light that may or may not come.

The long-term bull case is credible. SoFi has built what the traditional banking industry could not: a fully digital, fully regulated national bank with 15 million engaged members who are using more products every quarter. The SoFiUSD stablecoin launch is not a gimmick — it is the first time any US national bank has put a bank-grade stablecoin directly into the hands of retail customers at scale. If the crypto-banking convergence continues, SoFi has a structural first-mover advantage that no traditional bank can match without years of infrastructure build.

The near-term pain is also real. The Muddy Waters report and Block & Leviton investigation are not resolved and carry tail risk — if accounting irregularities are substantiated, the stock could test $10. Investors with short time horizons (under 12 months) should treat this as speculative until legal clarity emerges. For patient investors with a 2–3 year view who believe in the digital banking thesis, a gradual position at current prices — sized to tolerate a 30–40% drawdown in the bear case — offers an asymmetric risk-reward profile. Watch the Q2 2026 earnings (late July) for any update on the legal situation and free cash flow trajectory.
