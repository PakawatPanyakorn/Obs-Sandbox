# solarpunk-utopia

Eco-futuristic optimism built on a literal photosynthesis palette — living green, solar yellow, warm orange — where technology and nature are depicted as cooperating, not opposing.

## Core Concept
Living-mint near-white canvas (`#F0FCE8`) under a 3-layer organic radial gradient explicitly described as a "photosynthesis colour palette" (green biome + solar yellow + warm orange). Anybody's variable-width display face against Figtree's clean humanist sans. Fully rounded pill buttons (`buttonRadius: 999px`) and generous card radius (`16px`) signal softness and approachability; `primary` and `success` share one hex (`#2A8C3A`) — in this theme, "the brand color" and "things are healthy/growing" are the same statement, reinforcing the ecological-optimism premise.

## Color Role Guidance

### primary (`#2A8C3A` living green) — also `success`
- When to use: the dominant "growth/life" signal — primary buttons (`--gradient-button`, green gradient), the largest background gradient layer, glow color. Its identity with success means green always carries a "things are working, ecologically" connotation.
- Surface area: generous — green anchors the visual system throughout.
- Don't: don't introduce a separate success color distinct from primary — the collapse is intentional and central to the "utopia" framing (thriving = the brand color itself).

### secondary (`#D4B200` solar yellow)
- When to use: the "solar power" note — one gradient stop (`--gradient-text` runs green→yellow), a warm energy signal distinct from caution.
- Surface area: moderate — a genuine second voice representing energy/sun, used deliberately, not just as a warning-adjacent color.
- Don't: don't conflate secondary with `warning` (`#C09000`, a duller gold) — secondary represents solar energy/optimism, warning is a genuine caution state; keep them visually distinguishable.

### accent (`#F07020` warm orange)
- When to use: the "warmth/harvest" note — gradient endpoints (`--gradient-accent` runs green→orange), a human-warmth counterpoint to the cooler green/yellow pairing.
- Surface area: small — a highlight, not a fill; the third and rarest of the photosynthesis-palette colors.
- Don't: don't use accent interchangeably with secondary — orange specifically signals warmth/harvest, yellow specifically signals solar/energy; they're different notes in the same ecological metaphor.

## When To Use
- Sustainability, clean-tech, community/cooperative, or eco-futurist content — the theme's own ecological optimism is literal, not just a green color scheme.
- Products wanting genuine warmth and approachability (rounded pills, soft radii) combined with a forward-looking, hopeful register.
- Content that benefits from `hierarchyClarity: high` with `expressive` motion — active, energetic, but still clear.

## When NOT To Use
- Corporate, clinical, or serious/urgent content — the rounded, optimistic register actively resists gravitas.
- Content needing a "success" state visually distinct from the brand color — this theme's collapse of primary/success means a target needing that distinction will need adjustment.
- Cold or minimalist branding — this is a warm, gradient-forward, softly-shaped theme throughout.

## How To Use — Full Potential
- Use the 3-layer organic radial gradient (green/yellow/orange) as a body-level ambient background, not confined to a hero — the "photosynthesis" effect needs to feel like atmosphere, not a decoration.
- Keep buttons fully rounded (999px) consistently — the pill shape is as much a signal of "soft, cooperative future" as the color palette itself.
- Reserve the 3-color gradient border (green→yellow→orange) for a single hero or feature panel — the one place the full photosynthesis metaphor appears together.
- If only one thing: apply a fully-rounded green button with the green→yellow gradient text nearby — the fastest way to signal solarpunk-utopia's optimistic ecological register.

## Apply-Mode Notes
- Step 4a: preserve primary=success — don't introduce a separate success color during harmonize; the collapse is intentional and reinforces the theme's "thriving = brand color" premise.
- Step 4c (shape): keep buttons/cards fully rounded — sharp corners actively contradict this theme's soft, optimistic identity.
