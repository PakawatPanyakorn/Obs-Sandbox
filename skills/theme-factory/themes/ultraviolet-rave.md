# ultraviolet-rave

Genuine blacklight-club illumination — UV violet, hot pink, and acid green glowing against pure dark, calibrated as if lit by actual UV lamps rather than a generic neon-purple gesture.

## Core Concept
Deep purple-black canvas (`#0D0818`) under a 4-layer radial gradient (violet, hot pink, acid green, deep indigo) explicitly framed as "blacklight club illumination on pure dark." Megrim's distinctive thin geometric display face against Raleway's ultra-light (200wt) body — an unusually delicate typographic pairing for such a maximalist color system, evoking glow-stick-thin lettering rather than bold neon signage. `accent` and `success` share one hex (`#00FF88`) — the acid green that reads as "UV-reactive" also reads as "confirmed/good," reinforcing the rave's implicit "the party is working" energy.

## Color Role Guidance

### primary (`#CC00FF` UV violet)
- When to use: the dominant blacklight signal — primary buttons (`--gradient-button`), the strongest glow (up to 96px spread), headline text-shadow combining violet and pink.
- Surface area: generous — violet anchors the visual system throughout, appropriate for large glow-bearing surfaces.
- Don't: don't apply primary without heavy glow — every primary use carries multi-layer glow in the source preset; a flat violet fill reads as "the lights are off."

### secondary (`#FF0088` hot pink)
- When to use: a co-equal partner to violet — `--gradient-text` and headline text-shadow both pair secondary directly with primary.
- Surface area: substantial — as prominent as primary in gradients and glow combinations.
- Don't: don't demote secondary to a minor accent — the violet/pink pairing carries as much of the "rave" identity as violet alone.

### accent (`#00FF88` acid green) — also `success`
- When to use: the "UV-reactive glow-in-the-dark" note — the third stop in the 3-color gradient border and `--gradient-accent`, plus genuine success/confirmation states.
- Surface area: moderate — a bright, distinct third color that completes the UV triad; also does double duty as the confirmation color.
- Don't: don't introduce a separate success color — the collapse with accent is intentional; acid green already carries "good/confirmed" meaning.

## When To Use
- Music/event/festival, nightlife, or rave-culture content wanting genuine blacklight-club energy.
- Dark-mode products wanting maximal glow density with a specific UV/blacklight register, distinct from the cooler "deep space" glow of themes like [[cosmica]] or [[astral-drift]].
- Content that can sustain thin, delicate display type (Megrim) alongside heavy color/glow maximalism — an intentional contrast.

## When NOT To Use
- Calm, corporate, or understated content — every element (4-layer glow mesh, expressive motion, delicate-but-loud type) is built for high energy.
- Light-mode contexts — no light variant; the entire effect depends on pure dark "blacklight" illumination.
- Content needing 4+ genuinely distinct semantic colors — accent/success sharing a hex limits the palette to 3 chromatic roles plus warning/error.

## How To Use — Full Potential
- Use the 3-color gradient border (violet→pink→green) on a single hero or feature element — it's the fullest expression of the UV triad and loses impact if spread across every component.
- Pair Megrim's thin geometric letterforms with heavy glow text-shadow — the contrast between delicate type and maximal luminescence is what distinguishes this from a generic "bold neon" theme.
- Reserve accent/green specifically for confirmation or "this worked" moments, leaning into its dual accent/success role rather than treating it as a decorative third color.
- If only one thing: apply the violet+pink combined text-shadow (`0 0 20px rgba(204,0,255,0.7), 0 0 40px rgba(255,0,136,0.4)`) to a Megrim headline — the fastest way to signal ultraviolet-rave.

- Step 4j: multi-layer glow is structural, not optional — under-applying it during harmonize is the most common way this theme collapses into a generic dark-purple theme.
