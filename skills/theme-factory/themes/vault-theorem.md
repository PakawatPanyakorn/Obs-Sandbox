# vault-theorem

Wealth-management institutional finance — navy, burnished copper, and electric violet on soft lavender, distinguished by a genuinely dark navy hero panel embedded within an otherwise light theme.

## Core Concept
Soft lavender canvas (`#F0EBF8`) under a diamond crosshatch lattice (23px, navy-tinted) plus a 4-layer gradient overlay. Josefin Slab's geometric slab-serif display against Spectral's refined optical serif body. Unlike sibling finance themes ([[meridian-capital]]'s champagne/cobalt-gold-rose, [[eigenvalue]]'s ice-lavender/violet-cyan-lime), vault-theorem's hero gradient (`--gradient-hero`) is a genuinely **dark navy-on-navy** treatment (`#1C2E58 → #141E3C → #1C2A50`) — a deliberate dark panel embedded within the light theme, evoking a bank vault door or an institutional dark-mode terminal insert, not just a tinted light gradient.

## Color Role Guidance

### primary (`#1C2E58` midnight navy)
- When to use: institutional authority — the dark hero panel, primary buttons (`--gradient-button`, a navy-on-navy gradient), the gradient border's starting anchor.
- Surface area: generous, including the one genuinely dark section (the hero) — this theme is unusual in having a real dark panel inside a light overall theme.
- Don't: don't lighten primary's hero treatment into a pastel — the dark navy panel is a deliberate "vault door" contrast against the lavender field, not an error to soften.

### secondary (`#C87830` burnished copper)
- When to use: the "wealth/metal" warm note — the middle stop in `--gradient-text` (navy→copper→violet), a tactile, precious-metal counterpoint to the cooler navy/violet.
- Surface area: moderate — bridges the palette in gradients, evokes vault fixtures/hardware.
- Don't: don't use copper as a primary action color — it's a warm accent within the institutional system, not the CTA color.

### accent (`#8B20FF` electric violet)
- When to use: the "strategic edge" note — gradient endpoints, one background mesh layer, a modern jolt against the otherwise traditional navy/copper pairing.
- Surface area: small to moderate — distinguishes this from a purely traditional finance theme by adding a genuinely electric, contemporary color.
- Don't: don't scale violet to dominate over navy — it's the "edge," not the institutional base.

## When To Use
- Wealth management, private banking, strategic finance, or institutional allocation content wanting a blend of traditional trust (navy) and contemporary edge (violet).
- Content that benefits from a real dark "vault" panel embedded in an otherwise light, approachable page — annual reports, portfolio dashboards with one dramatic dark section.
- Content wanting genuine differentiation from other finance-adjacent themes in the catalog via the navy/copper/violet-specific triad.

## When NOT To Use
- Content wanting a uniformly light OR uniformly dark theme — this theme's identity depends on the light/dark contrast between the lavender field and the navy hero.
- Casual or youthful branding — Josefin Slab's geometric precision and the institutional triad are formal.
- Content already using [[meridian-capital]] or [[eigenvalue]] elsewhere in the same project — the three finance-adjacent light themes are similar enough that mixing them risks visual confusion; pick one per project.

## How To Use — Full Potential
- Use the dark navy hero panel as a genuine embedded dark section, not just a tinted light gradient — this contrast is the theme's most distinctive trait relative to sibling finance themes.
- Apply the 3-stop gradient text (navy→copper→violet) to major headlines within the light sections — it visually bridges the institutional (navy), precious (copper), and contemporary (violet) notes in one move.
- Reserve the diamond crosshatch lattice as ambient texture across the full light-mode page — subtle enough to read as fine paper/security-printing texture.
- If only one thing: pair a dark navy hero panel with a lavender body section beneath it — the light/dark contrast alone is the fastest way to signal vault-theorem specifically.

## Apply-Mode Notes
- Step 4d: preserve the dark navy hero as a genuinely dark embedded panel — don't lighten it to match the rest of the light theme; the contrast is structural, not an inconsistency to fix.
- Step 4a: keep copper as a warm bridging accent, not a CTA color — action color should stay on navy or, secondarily, violet.
